Midnight × XRPL
Private Credential Gateway

Prove you qualify — privately. Let the XRP Ledger enforce it — natively.

The problem

Compliance vs. privacy

To access gated things you must prove you qualify — but proving usually means handing over your identity.

  • KYC-gated pools want proof you're verified — and collect your documents.
  • Age-gated commerce wants your birthday — and stores it.
  • Token-holder drops want your wallet — and dox it publicly.
The idea

Prove privately → enforce on-ledger

A zero-knowledge proof on Midnight becomes a native credential on XRPL — and the ledger itself does the gating.

No middleman holds your data. No server decides each payment. Private eligibility, public enforcement.

Architecture

Three pieces

① Midnight contract

The privacy layer. Holds a Merkle tree of valid credentials + a policy (e.g. "adult, in CA"). Verifies the ZK proof.

② Gateway service

The trusted issuer — a Certificate Authority. Holds an XRPL issuer key. Non-custodial: never touches user funds.

③ XRPL

The enforcement layer. Native Credentials + Deposit Authorization (live on mainnet). The ledger blocks or allows.

How it works

The flow

1
User holds a private credential — issued off-chain, its commitment is a leaf in the Midnight tree
2
Prove eligibility in zero-knowledge — membership + policy + binds to XRPL address; burns a nullifier (no reuse). Private data never goes on-chain.
3
Sign an XRPL challenge — proves the user controls the XRPL account being credentialed
4
Gateway verifies proof + signature → issues CredentialCreate
5
User submits CredentialAccept — credential is now live on their account
6
Gated account enforces it — Deposit Auth + credential preauth on every payment

Trust model

What you trust — and don't

✓ Trustless

The Midnight proof (the chain verifies it) and the XRPL enforcement (the ledger verifies the credential). No one can fake either.

⚖ Trusted (like a CA)

The gateway, to only issue for valid proofs. Risk is false-issuance, not theft — it never holds user funds.

Why it matters

A new primitive

Credentials on XRPL exist. Private proofs on Midnight exist. Wiring them together — prove privately, enforce on-ledger — is the new part.
